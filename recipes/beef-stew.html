Beef Stew

Beef stew is a hearty dish made by slow-cooking beef chunks with vegetables like carrots, and onions in a flavorful broth.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds beef stew meat, cut into 1 inch cubes
  • 2 teaspoons salt, divided
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper, divided
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cups water
    • <1i>(8 ounce) can whole peeled tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cubes beef bouillon
  • 1/8 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 6 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 6 medium carrrots, chopped
  • 6 stalks celery, chopped
  • 2 cups pearl onions

Directions on how to make beef stew

  1. Step 1

    2. Season beef with 1 teaspoon salt and 1/2 teaspoon pepper.

  2. Step 2

    3. Place flour in a resealable bag. Add seasoned beef, seal the bag, and toss to coat.

  3. Step 3

    4. Heat butter and oil in a large skillet over medium high heat. Add beef and onion; saute until beef is browned on all sides, 5 to 7 minutes. Transfer beef and onion to a large stockpot.

  4. Step 4

    5. Add water, tomatoes, parsley, garlic, bouillon cubes, remaining 1 teaspoon salt, remaining 1/2 teaspoon pepper, and thyme to the stockpot. Pour in wine and stir to combine. Cover and simmer for 1 1/2 hours.

  5. Step 5

    6. Add potatoes, carrots, celery, and pearl onions; continue to simmer until potatoes are tender, about 1 hour.
